Game Design Thinking!

Before I started making games, as a non-CS student, the biggest problem for me was coding. However, I was wrong. Actually, coding is the most obvious problem, but it is not the biggest one. Later I consulted this question with my friends who major in computer-related fields and the outcome of the interview confirmed my hypothesis. They said Art or Story are the most essential things.

Thatâ€™s interesting. Countless games have imperfections here or there: nonsense story, strange art, weird game mechanisms, not to mention eternal bugs. However, these defections just can not prevent these games attractive! You might think â€œOK, these games are attractive because they also have some prosâ€. I am afraid to reject this opinion.

Last Saturday I visited the Hong Kong Maritime Museum. There is an arcade cabinet decorated with a container on deck B. Players use a console to simulate a crane operator in an arcade. In this game player should place containers on ship deck, if too many containers weigh the ship loses balance or fails to load all containers in 2 minutes, players will lose the game. TBH, that sounds boring. But my mom (a typical Asian parent who is never into video games at all!) and I just canâ€™t quit! She played the game with passion and patience even her ship sank repeatedly. The boy line after her almost protests for her long occupied.

That started my thinking about game-level design. HOW CAN I MAKE IT FUN?
